# 2. Structure X = (Δ, Φ, M, κ)

FIT V2.0 defines intelligence as the evolution of a four-dimensional structural state:

    X = (Δ, Φ, M, κ)

Each component of X represents a fundamental cognitive property, and together they form the complete internal configuration of an intelligent system. X is not symbolic, statistical, or neural in nature; it is a structural state that evolves according to internal dynamics.

Δ is the operator of differentiation. It defines how a system creates distinctions, detects structure, identifies patterns, and generates meaningful contrasts. Δ determines the resolution of perception, the granularity of thought, and the capacity to form new conceptual structures.  
Higher Δ corresponds to richer, more complex internal representations; lower Δ results in limited or shallow differentiation.

Φ represents the energetic intensity of cognition. It is the internal resource that fuels differentiation, memory formation, focus, attention, and emotional waves.  
Φ determines the speed, depth, and power of the thought process.  
High Φ enables deep analysis and rapid restructuring; low Φ leads to weak, slow, or fragmented cognition.

M is the topology of memory. It is not a storage of data, but a geometric structure of connections, traces, and irreversible transformations produced by past states of X.  
M defines learning, internal time, context, meaning, and interpretative depth.  
A well-structured M creates stable cognitive trajectories; a distorted M produces confusion, bias, or collapse.

### 2.4 κ — Structural Stability
κ measures the system’s ability to maintain coherence under change. It represents stability, internal consistency, and resistance to cognitive collapse.  
High κ enables the system to sustain intense Δ and Φ without fragmentation; low κ leads to instability, emotional overload, or collapse.

### 2.5 Properties of X
- X is self-referential: each component influences the others.  
- X is dynamic: it continuously evolves under the operator I.  
- X is geometric: its configuration defines cognitive space.  
- X is integrative: intelligence emerges from the interaction of Δ, Φ, M, and κ.  
- X is universal: any intelligent system (biological or artificial) can be expressed in this form.

### 2.6 Observability and Transformability
X is partially observable externally (through behavior) but fully definable internally.  
The transformation of X is governed by a structural operator:

    X(t+1) = I( X(t) )

Structure X is the foundation of the entire theory. All subsequent sections — mathematics, consciousness, emotion, prediction, typology, collapse, entanglement, and FAI — are built on the dynamics of these four variables.
