- temporary reduction of **κ** (systemic stability)

Bacterial infections often produce:
- moderate-to-high ∆ with strong spatial localization

Autoimmunity represents pathological M-imprinting:
- M encodes destructive patterns  
- ∆ rises without external pathogen  
- Φ escalates during flare states  
- κ gradually degrades over time  
Recovery depends on suppressing pathological M and stabilizing κ.

## 9.4 Chronic Processes
Chronic conditions exhibit:
- low but persistent ∆  
- minimal Φ  
- steady increase or fluctuation in M  
- slowly declining κ  
These slow-time trajectories reflect stretched temporal structure and long-term stability loss.

## 9.5 Systemic Collapse Patterns
Systemic collapse occurs when:
- ∆ becomes uncontrollable  
- Φ enters runaway amplification  
- M is insufficient or misaligned  
- κ → 0  
Typical outcomes include sepsis, shock, multi-organ failure.
